If one thing really amazed people in the original Grand Theft Auto it was the audio - great music, and a really noisey city combined to make one of the most responsive and entertaining aural environments yet unleashed on video games. Then GTA: London 1969 re-inforced this point by winning a Bafta for Best Audio with it's combination of cockney charm and West London reggae. Now it's GTA2's turn. An all new audio engine, a host of personality disc jockeys, and over two hours of original and classic music, including two original mixes by Moving Shadow records. Here Colin Anderson and Craig Connor from the legendary DMA Audio department fill us in. Then in the music section, we give you an exclusive preview of the game's audio possibilities.

Everyone wants GTA2 to sound as good as it possibly can, and no one wants that more so than the Audio Department.

We are using several technologies we have developed here at DMA which no one else in the industry is using that will make the audio in GTA2 significantly better than it's predecessor. Unfortunately this has a side-effect and that is it creates substantially more work, and therefore takes considerably more time, to design the sounds and implement them properly in the game.

For example, in the original GTA only 1 sample was used to generate the sound of each vehicle.

By comparison in GTA 2 we are balancing 6 different sounds for each vehicle; engine idling, engine revving, gear noise, tyre noise, close wind noise and distant wind noise. This is more than 6 times the work, because not only do 6 times the number of samples have to be generated per vehicle but each one behaves differently as the car manoeuvres and so has to be controlled separately.

This is the level of detail we are applying to all aspects of the audio. In addition to all the better quality sound effects that will be implemented in the game here is a summary of the enhanced audio features we intend to implement in the PC version of GTA 2:

  • An updated version of the DMAudio engine as featured in Wild Metal Country.
  • Dynamic Sample Layering (DSL) on objects.
  • Support for all major 3D sound APIs - (Aureal, Creative Labs EAX, Dolby Pro-Logic)
  • Enhanced Police crime reporting system.
  • More than an hour of brand new music modified to give an authentic 'radio' sound.
  • Radio Stations based on geographical location and gang alliance.
  • No pause when entering or exiting a vehicle.
  • Different pedestrian comments depending on your status with a particular gang.

An updated version of the DMAudio engine as featured in Wild Metal Country.

Our new DMAudio engine which was used for the first time in Wild Metal Country is the result of experience gained from implementing audio in DMA's titles over the past 6 years. It successfully addresses most of the problems that are encountered by teams when trying to make games sound good by allowing the Audio Programmers unhindered access to all the game variables necessary for accurate control of sound.

This means the Audio Team have full control over all aspects of the in-game sound which results in a better sounding product.

Dynamic Sample Layering (DSL) on objects.

Most objects in GTA 2 will be represented by what we call 'Compound Sounds'. These are sounds perceived by the listener as one sound but which are actually made up of multiple samples. An example from Wild Metal Country would be the player's tank. Although the player hears the sound as 'a tank' it is actually composed of individual, engine, gearbox, track, surface and suspension samples.

The relative levels of each sample are varied with distance so that the resulting sound is far more convincing than would otherwise be achievable. The cars in GTA 2 will benefit most from this.

Support for all major 3D sound APIs - (Aureal, Creative Labs EAX, Dolby Pro-Logic)

GTA2 will provide a full 3D soundscape for anyone with the appropriate hardware. Aureal said after hearing the audio in Wild Metal Country "Wild Metal Country is indeed a wonderful aural experience! Great work!!". We've learned a lot from this and intend to make GTA2 even better.

Enhanced Police crime reporting system.

Crimes will be reported with a more informative structure enhancing the player's illusion that he is being tracked by the police. In addition to reporting the location and type of crime committed we will also be able to announce the colour and type of vehicle the suspect was last seen in and the direction in which he was headed.

e.g. "All units in vicinity of north west Island City respond to a 1014. Suspect is heading west in a light green saloon."

This will be tied in to the player aquiring a 'wanted' level. At other times a simpler "There's a 1024 in south Hackenslash" will suffice. This gives an audio cue of the player's wanted level increasing as well as the visual feedback of another police head appearing at the top of the screen.

More than an hour of brand new music modified to give an authentic 'radio' sound.

All the music in the game will be treated to sound as if it is being played back from a car radio. This will add more to the illusion of being in a vehicle than the music in the original GTA which, although effective, sounded like a CD being played over the top of the game.

Radio Stations based on geographical location and gang alliance.

The different radio stations for different type of vehicle was a popular feature of the original game. This time we will have radio stations physically located within different areas of the city. Stations on gang turf will play hardcore gang music and will only broadcast over limited range whereas the large commercial stations will broadcast over the whole city playing their sickly-sweet pop tunes.

No pause when entering or exiting a vehicle.

By using a system of streamed Wav files rather than purely playing a CD along with the game we have managed to eliminate the irritating pause that occurred on some machines in the original game when a player stepped in or out of a vehicle. It also allows us to maintain a realtime element to radio stations by enabling the tracks to keep playing after the player leaves a vehicle so that when he returns to a vehicle with the same station it will no longer be playing the same song.

It should also be noted that the music will not be able to be played from a normal CD player. This could be a benefit if it was deemed appropriate to release a soundtrack CD later on.

Different pedestrian comments depending on your status with a particular gang.

Gang peds will shout different comments at the player depending on his respect level. Friendly gangs will shout encouraging remarks like "Go get 'em buddy" while rival gangs will shout "Get the f**ker" before opening fire
